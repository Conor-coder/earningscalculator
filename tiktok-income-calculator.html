How to actually get there

5 things that separate growing accounts from stalling ones

  1. Post consistently — volume beats perfection early on

    Aim for at least 5 posts per week. The algorithm rewards accounts that publish on a predictable schedule, and most viral breakthroughs are preceded by dozens of ordinary videos. Ship, analyse, adjust.

  2. Pick one niche and own it before you branch out

    A channel about “personal finance tips for UK 20-somethings” grows faster than one mixing finance, cooking, and lifestyle. The algorithm builds a niche audience for you when you stay consistent on a topic.

  3. Write your hook before your script

    Your first 2 seconds decide whether someone scrolls or watches. A bold claim, a surprising stat, or an unresolved question pulls viewers in. Test 3 different openers for the same idea and see which one retains more viewers past the 3-second mark.

  4. Track completion rate, not just view count

    If viewers drop off in the first 5 seconds, TikTok stops pushing your video. Aim for 60%+ average watch-through. Videos with high completion get amplified; low completion gets buried — regardless of how many followers you have.

  5. Start building off-platform from day one

    Point engaged followers to an email list or a second platform. A following you can export means algorithm changes and account restrictions don’t wipe your business. Treat TikTok as a funnel, not the destination.
